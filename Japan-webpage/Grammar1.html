    N1 は N2 です。
    Particle は
    The particle は indicates that the word before it is the topic of the sentece. You select a noun you want to talk about, add は to show that it is the topic and give a statement about the topic.

    Note: は is read "wa", not "ha"

    です
    Nouns used with です work as predicates. です indicates judgement ot assertion. It also conveys thet the speaker is being polite towards the listener. です inflects when the sentece is negative or in the past tense.
    ~さん
    San is certainly the first honorific title you will learn when studying Japanese or going to Japan. The usage of san is very wide: the suffix can be added to a person’s characteristic and entities such as a company.

    One common mistake Japanese learners make is referring to themselves with an honorific title. Remember to never use san or any other title to refer to yourself unless you want to sound arrogant! The only exception to the rule is chan: in a casual context, some people, in particular, women, will refer to themselves in a childish third person.

    N は N じゃ（では）ありません
    じゃ（では）ありません is the negative form of です. It is the form used in daily conversation. For a formal speech or writing, では ありません is used instead.

    Note: では is read "dewa", not "deha"

    Sか
    Particle か
    The particle か is used to express the speaker's doubt, question, uncertainty, etc. A question is formed by simply adding か to the end of the sentence. A question ends with a rising intonation.
    Questions asking whether a statement is correct or not. Depending on whether you agree with the statement or not, your answer to such a question begins with　はい or いいえ.

    • わたし　は　ふじだいがく　　がくせいです。
    • ワンさん　は　さくらだいがく　の　せんせいです。
    N1 の N2
    の is used to connect two nouns. N1 modifies N2. N1 is an organization or some kind of group to which N2 belongs.

    • サントスさん　は　ブラジルじん です。

    N も
    も is added after a topic instead of は when the statement about the topic is the same as the previous topic.
